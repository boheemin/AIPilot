Part 2: Understanding Generative AI and Large Language Models
Duration: 20 minutes

🤖 What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can create new content—text, images, code, or other media—based on patterns learned from vast amounts of training data. Unlike traditional AI that simply classifies or analyzes existing content, generative AI can produce original outputs that didn't exist before.

🔑 Key Characteristics

  • Creative Output: Generates new content rather than just analyzing existing data
  • Pattern Recognition: Learns from massive datasets to understand language, style, and context
  • Versatile Applications: Can assist with writing, research, analysis, and problem-solving
  • Interactive: Responds to natural language prompts and can engage in conversations

📹 Generative AI explained in 2 minutes

🧠 What are Large Language Models (LLMs)?

Large Language Models are a specific type of generative AI trained on enormous amounts of text data. They understand and generate human-like text by predicting the most likely next word or phrase based on context. Popular examples include ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

⚙️ How LLMs Work (Simplified)

📚
1. Training
Learn from billions of text examples from books, websites, and articles
🔍
2. Pattern Recognition
Identify relationships between words, concepts, and contexts
✨
3. Generation
Predict and produce appropriate, contextual responses

Part 3: Responsible AI Use & Critical Thinking
Duration: 10 minutes

🔍 Critical Evaluation Framework

As Philosophy of Science and Qualitative Methods students, you're uniquely positioned to critically assess AI outputs using rigorous analytical frameworks.

⚠️ Key Considerations

  • Bias Recognition: AI may miss cultural context in qualitative data
  • Source Verification: Always validate AI-suggested sources
  • Academic Integrity: Proper citation of AI assistance required
  • Critical Distance: AI enhances but never replaces your judgment

📹 Responsible AI Use

📹 AI Ethics in Practice

🚨 Recognizing AI Limitations

Develop critical AI evaluation skills through these actionable steps:

✅ Always cross-verify AI-suggested sources
Check that citations exist and are accurately represented
✅ Question AI assumptions
Ask "What perspectives might be missing from this response?"
✅ Test AI consistency
Ask the same question multiple ways to identify contradictions
✅ Recognize knowledge cutoffs
Be aware that AI training has date limitations
✅ Check for cultural bias
Consider whether responses reflect diverse viewpoints
✅ Validate factual claims
Don't accept statistics or data without independent verification
✅ Maintain critical distance
Use AI outputs as starting points, not final answers. Your critical thinking and subject expertise remain essential.
